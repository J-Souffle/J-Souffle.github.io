About Me

Hello! I'm Joshua Cancio, an enthusiastic and committed student on a path to earn my degree in Computer Science. My passion lies in software engineering, and I have set my sights on a fulfilling career in this field. Throughout my life, my main goals are self-improvement, continuous learning, and striving to be the best version of myself. My fascination with computers began at an early age. In middle school, I was introduced to the world of programming, and it immediately captured my interest. I embarked on this journey by creating games using Scratch, ventured into web development, and then delved into the fundamentals of Java. Over the years, my skills and enthusiasm grew, leading me to assemble computers, experiment with circuitry, and develop practical programs to automate daily tasks. When the time came to select my major for university, I faced a significant decision between IT, computer science, and cybersecurity. Ultimately, I chose computer science, driven by my ambition to grasp more intricate coding techniques and leverage my six years of programming experience to become a proficient developer. Moreover, the realm of machine learning and artificial intelligence has always intrigued me. I firmly believe that the integration of AI into our society holds incredible potential, making software development as a Computer Science major the ideal path for me to immerse myself in my greatest passions within this field. While my enthusiasm for the hardware aspects of computers remains, my current academic focus primarily centers on software and data sciences. Beyond the world of computers, I have a deep passion for sports like volleyball and basketball, along with a developed skill for playing two musical instruments—the piano and saxophone. Spending quality time with my friends and family is equally important to me. Around my numerous career aspirations and professional goals, I value the importance of leading a well-rounded life. Every day, I strive for self-improvement and commit myself to embracing and learning from the diverse experiences that life's journey offers.
